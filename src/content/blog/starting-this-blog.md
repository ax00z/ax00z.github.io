I'm currently looking for a full-time dev job. That's the honest starting point.

I'm not writing this for sympathy. The market is rough, and I know the drill: everyone's fighting for the same entry-level roles, companies want 3 years of experience for junior positions, and the hiring process feels designed to waste your time. None of that is new.

What I can control is what I do with the time I have. So I'm building things, studying fundamentals, and now, writing about it.

## Why a blog

A few reasons:

**It forces clarity.** When I study something like hash maps or system design patterns, I can fool myself into thinking I understand it after watching a video. Writing about it is different. If I can't explain it clearly in my own words, I don't actually know it.

**It's a public record.** Recruiters and hiring managers can see my GitHub, but code alone doesn't always tell the full story. A blog post that walks through my thinking (why I made certain decisions, what tradeoffs I considered, what I'd do differently) says a lot more than a README.

**It keeps me accountable.** Having a site I need to update gives me a reason to keep shipping, even on the weeks where the job search feels pointless.

## What to expect

I'll be writing about three things:

1. **Project breakdowns**: what I built, the technical decisions, lessons learned
2. **CS fundamentals**: data structures, algorithms, system design, explained the way I wish they'd been explained to me
3. **Career stuff**: the job search, interview prep, things I figure out along the way

No fixed schedule. I'll post when I have something worth posting. Quality over frequency.

## Where I'm at

Right now I'm deep in interview prep: revisiting data structures, grinding algorithm problems, and reviewing system design concepts. I'm also working on a couple of side projects that I'll write about soon.

If you're in a similar spot (looking for that next role, trying to stay sharp while the market figures itself out), hopefully some of this is useful. And if you're a hiring manager reading this, well, I'm available.

Let's go.
